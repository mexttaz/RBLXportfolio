A Fully Functional Alternative Competitive Stand-Based Combat Game

This portfolio is built mainly around me and my studio's upcoming game, Brainrot Rivals — a full client/server framework. A few examples that just scratch the surface: hit detection, blocking, and parries all live on the server; characters share a common ability architecture instead of duplicated, spaghetti-slop logic; and matches run through a real queue → round → reward pipeline with reconnect and forfeit handling. Spotlight One

Combat Framework

Real, responsive and immersive combat with stand-based abilities, blocking, parries, and grabs — all decided on the server to prevent exploits, A.K.A so it can't be faked from the client.

I built the server-authoritative combat layer covering damage application, hit detection, hit-stun, i-frames, knockback, blocking, parry / guard-break, recovery windows, combo gating, and team / friendly-fire filtering.

CombatServiceBlockServiceGrabServiceBRivalsEngineCoreStandManager

Block & Parry

Making risky combat actually feel risky: land into a parry and you're stunned for a split second — for experienced players, that's all it takes to turn the fight.

Hit-Stun & Endlag

Every hit and whiffed attack carries real recovery time, so committing to an attack is a genuine risk — punishing a missed swing or following up on a landed hit is where actual skill expression happens.

Hit Detection & Damage Application

Every hit is validated server-side against hitboxes and attack data before any damage lands, so what you see connect is what actually counts — no client-side guesswork, no desync.

Friendly Fire Filtering

Team-aware filtering keeps abilities from hitting your own teammates, so area attacks and crowd-control abilities stay useful without accidentally griefing your own team.

Spotlight Two

Character & Ability System

17 playable characters, each with a full kit of unique abilities, built on one shared framework instead of a massive buggy script.

I designed the shared controller / state / ability-module pattern every character follows, and personally built 10 of the 17 character kits (part of the project's ~90 total ability modules) on top of it; the remaining characters were built by another scripter using the same framework I built.

ControllerStateM1 / M2 / E / R / T / X modules

Ability Uniqueness

Each character has its own moveset, built around a distinct combat identity — no two kits feel the same, and no ability is just a recolored copy of another.

Visual Uniqueness

Each character has its own cosmetics and skins, so swapping characters changes how the game looks and feels, not just what buttons do.

Scalable Roster

Because every character follows the same controller / state / ability-module pattern, adding a new character means writing new abilities, not rewriting combat — the roster grew without the codebase getting messier.

Spotlight Three

Gacha, Inventory & Cosmetics

A balanced roll-for-a-character economy, plus the inventory and cosmetics that show off what players unlock.

Built the rarity-pool rolling system for characters, cosmetics, perks, and pets, along with the inventory and rolling UI, live viewport previews, immersive cards with dynamic character poses showing off owned cosmetics, and cosmetic rig application on the player's character.

StandRegistryCosmeticRegistryInventoryControllerV2RollStandController

Rarity-Weighted Rolls

Characters, cosmetics, perks, and pets all roll from configured rarity pools, so the economy stays tunable from one place instead of hardcoded odds scattered across scripts.

Live Viewport Previews

Every roll and inventory item renders in a real 3D viewport instead of a flat icon, so players see exactly what they're getting before they commit to using it.

Dynamic Pose Cards

Inventory cards show each character posed dynamically with their equipped cosmetics applied, turning a plain item list into something that actually shows off what you've unlocked.

Cosmetic Rig Application

Cosmetics apply directly onto the player's character rig in real time, so what's equipped in the menu is exactly what shows up in a match.

Spotlight Five

Competitive Matchmaking

Real queues and real matches — lobby to round to scoreboard, with reconnect and forfeit handled instead of just breaking.

Built the full competitive flow: MemoryStore-backed lobby queues, match tickets, reserved-server teleportation, round lifecycle, scoring, MVP tracking, spectator mode, reconnect, and forfeit handling.

MatchmakingServiceRoundServiceScoreServiceReconnectService

Queueing & Match Tickets

Lobby queues are backed by MemoryStore, with match tickets used to safely group players into a match without race conditions or duplicate joins.

Round Lifecycle & Scoring

Matches run through a full round lifecycle — start, scoring, win conditions — handled server-side so results can't be tampered with from the client.

MVP & Spectator Mode

Standout performances get tracked and surfaced as MVP, and eliminated or disconnected players can drop into spectator mode instead of just sitting on a black screen.

Reconnect & Forfeit Handling

Dropped connections don't end the match — players can reconnect into their ongoing game, and matches that can't recover resolve cleanly through forfeit handling instead of breaking.
